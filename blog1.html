Does the early church which was established in bible encourage tithe?

In the early Christian church, as depicted in the New Testament, there is no explicit mandate for believers to practice tithing, which is the act of giving 10% of one's income or produce. Instead, the early Christians emphasized voluntary, generous, and communal support.

Key Practices in the Early Church:

  • Voluntary Giving: The early Christians were encouraged to give according to their means, without a specified percentage. The Apostle Paul, in his letters, urged believers to set aside a portion of their income willingly and cheerfully to support the community and those in need.
  • Communal Sharing: The Book of Acts describes how early believers shared their possessions to ensure no one among them lacked necessities. This communal approach reflects a spirit of generosity and mutual care rather than adherence to a fixed tithing requirement

Historical Development:

  • Post-Apostolic Period: As the church grew, discussions about financial support emerged. Some early church fathers advocated for systematic giving, but there was no universal enforcement of tithing.

In summary, while the early Christian church did not mandate tithing, it fostered a culture of generous and voluntary giving to support the community and its mission. The formal practice of tithing evolved over time, becoming more established in church tradition centuries after the apostolic era.
